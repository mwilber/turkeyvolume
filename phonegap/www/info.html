Using the size of an average Thanksgiving turkey as a base unit, every space can be measured in terms of how many turkeys will fill it. The Turkey Volume system was first proposed in the television series Mystery Science Theater 3000 as a superior method of measuring volume. Though actual turkeys may vary greatly in size, the Turkey Volume system is based on a standard roughly equivalent to 1.5 cubic feet (0.04 cubic meters). The Turkey Volume system is best used for measuring enclosed spaces, such as the size of your turkey storage freezer.


“Turkey Volume Guessing Man” in MST3k episode “Riding With Death”

The Turkey Volume system was later adapted to suit the modern vegan movement. This adaptation uses the average size of a tofu turkey, roughly equivalent to 0.75 cubic feet (0.02 cubic meters). Though this method is widely used, it is not accepted as an official measurement system.
